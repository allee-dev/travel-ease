Gilgit-Baltistan

Home to some of the world's highest peaks including K2, Gilgit-Baltistan offers unparalleled mountain scenery, ancient cultures, and adventure opportunities. This region is where three great mountain ranges meet: the Himalayas, Karakoram, and Hindu Kush.

Hunza Valley

The crown jewel of Northern Pakistan, Hunza Valley offers stunning views of Rakaposhi and Ultar peaks, ancient forts, and the famous longevity of its people. Known for its apricot blossoms and terraced fields.

Best Time
March - October
Elevation
2,400m

Major Attractions

  • Altit Fort (1,000 years old)
  • Baltit Fort with museum
  • Attabad Lake (turquoise waters)
  • Passu Cones (cathedral peaks)
  • Khunjerab Pass (China border)

Culture & Food

Experience Hunzakut hospitality with traditional dishes like Hunza bread, apricot oil, and glacier water. The region is famous for its organic farming and unique Wakhi culture.

Budget: $30-80/day

Skardu

Gateway to the world's second-highest peak K2, Skardu is a paradise for mountaineers and nature lovers. The region offers dramatic landscapes, crystal-clear lakes, and ancient Buddhist heritage.

Best Time
May - September
Elevation
2,230m

Major Attractions

  • Shangrila Resort (Heaven on Earth)
  • Sheosar Lake (alpine paradise)
  • Katpana Desert (cold desert)
  • Skardu Fort (ancient fortress)
  • Satpara Lake (emerald waters)

Culture & Food

Rich Balti culture with influences from Tibet and Central Asia. Try traditional Balti cuisine including chapshuro, mamtu, and butter tea while enjoying local music and dance.

Budget: $35-90/day

Khaplu

A hidden gem in the Shyok Valley, Khaplu offers pristine nature, traditional architecture, and access to some of the most remote trekking routes in the Karakoram range.

Best Time
May - October
Elevation
2,600m

Major Attractions

  • Khaplu Palace (royal heritage)
  • Chaqchan Mosque (700 years old)
  • Shyok River Valley
  • Mashabrum View Point
  • Trekking routes to Hushe and Gondogoro La

Culture & Food

Khaplu blends Tibetan and Central Asian culture. Enjoy dishes like buckwheat noodles, balay soup, and yak butter tea. The region is known for its wood-carved homes and peaceful vibes.

Budget: $25-70/day

Khyber Pakhtunkhwa (KPK)

Known for its lush green valleys, majestic rivers, and unique Pashtun culture, KPK is a vibrant travel destination. Swat, Kalam, and Chitral are among its most famous spots, offering beauty and serenity.

Swat Valley

Often called the "Switzerland of the East", Swat boasts lush valleys, waterfalls, and ancient Buddhist heritage. A family-friendly destination for all seasons.

Best Time
April - September
Elevation
980m - 2,100m

Major Attractions

  • Malam Jabba (ski resort)
  • Kalam Valley
  • Mahodand Lake
  • Butkara Stupa
  • Ushu Forest

Culture & Food

Enjoy authentic Pashtun cuisine like chapli kebab and seekh kebab. Swat is also known for handcrafted shawls and embroidery.

Budget: $20-50/day

Kalash Valley

Nestled in Chitral, Kalash Valley is home to the indigenous Kalash people with their own language, traditions, and colorful festivals.

Best Time
May - September
Elevation
1,670m

Major Attractions

  • Bumburet Valley
  • Rumbur Village
  • Traditional Kalash Homes
  • Chilimjusht Festival

Culture & Food

Kalash people follow a polytheistic religion and celebrate with dance, wine, and colorful attire. Try Kalash cheese and walnut bread during your stay.

Budget: $25-60/day
